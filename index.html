Executive summary

What leadership needs to know before scrolling the tables.

  • Residential coverage: 99.99% of county residential baseline parcels (453,215 of 453,276) appear in PostgreSQL public.unidata when both sides share the same normalized scity and parcel number (parcelnumb, trimmed and case-normalized).
  • Unmatched baseline: 61 residential baseline rows still lack that combined match—often scity label drift or missing ingest rows rather than literal absence of the parcel everywhere.
  • Where we measure attributes: The cards in Table 3 summarize one row count per normalized scity present in Unidata—34 buckets in this snapshot.
  • Soft spots in the schema: The weakest populated column overall right now is fhszsra (2.43%) (see Table 5 for more).

The KPI tiles directly above mirror these bullets; the How to read each table section next defines every column.
